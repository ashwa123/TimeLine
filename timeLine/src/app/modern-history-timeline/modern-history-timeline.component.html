Indian press act of 1910

Imposing strict censorship on all kinds of publications

  • Proprietors to deposit 500 to 5000 Rupees as the "Magistrate" saw fit.
  • Deposit Security for registration & submit 2 copies at Local government
  • "Customs and postal officers" power to detain and examine suspected matter.
  • "Local governments" power to declare forfeit any newspaper, book, or document, or empower the police to search and seize the same.
  • This act defined "Press offences" in the bill.
  • Repealed by Lord Reading in 1921
