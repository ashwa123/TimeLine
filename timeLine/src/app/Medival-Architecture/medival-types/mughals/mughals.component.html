    • Calligraphy + Pietra Dura + Charbagh garden + Foresightening technique
    • Jaali Works -"Extremely fine & lace like"
    • Marble carvings were low relief [Clay is removed or added to strategic areas which play with light and shadows, thus creating an illusion of superficial depth]

Grand Trunk Road / Sadak-e-Azam

Grand Trunk Road names during each period

  • Built by Chandragupta Maurya
  • Mauryan Empire: "Uttarpatha" [East India to Ancient Greece & Central Asia]
  • Sher Shan: renamed it as "Sadak-e-Azam" [Breadth increased Further rerouted to "Sonargaon-Bangladesh" to "Rohtas-Pakistan" ]
  • Mughals: "Badshahi Sadak". Jahangir built all sarais into bricks & stone.
  • British: "Grand Trunk Road" [Calcutta - Delhi -Kabul, Afghan]
  • India: UNESCO - Tentative list
