Mistakes to be avoided to score better:


  • Read the names of association completely and carefully. For example, don’t confuse between Association, League, Sabha and Society.
    Read BIA-British India Association as it is, don’t get confused with other names like British Indian Society.
  • Read the names of leaders completely and carefully including initials. Sometimes MG Ranade will be asked as Mahadeo Govind Ranade. So read the initials carefully, so that you can match the names.
  • Colour Differentiation: Places (Make use of the colour differentiation in the content so that it is easy to remember places). Example is shared below.
    Bengal-green
    Bombay-blue
    Madras-pink
  • You can remember the places of origin by seeing the second name of founders. For example, Banerjea-Bengal, Iyer-Madras, Ranade-Bombay (not in all cases).
  • After reading the content once, just go through the bold letters to revise. So that you can revise quickly (Bold letters are the ones that are the most important, so don’t ignore it).
  • Link the leaders names to your friends names, so that it is easy for visualization.
  • History is full of facts and is easily forgotten, so keep revising the content.
